The Power Trap: When the Church Trusts Itself More Than God

In today's modern church, we often see a shift from dependence on God to dependence on charisma, strategy, and self-made power. Worship becomes performance. Leadership becomes branding. Ministry becomes marketing. But Scripture warns us: self-reliance leads to spiritual ruin.

Example: King Uzziah

2 Chronicles 26 tells the story of King Uzziah, who was blessed by God and became powerful. But when he grew strong, his pride led him to enter the temple unlawfully, and God struck him with leprosy. His downfall came not from weakness‚Äîbut from strength without submission.

Reflection

Leaders

Are we building platforms or altars?

Worship Teams

Are we performing or ministering?

Congregations

Are we consuming or communing?

Let us return to the posture of repentance and rest. Let us trade self-exaltation for God-dependence.

"Lord, forgive us for trusting in our own strength. Teach us to rest in You, to lead with humility, and to serve with surrendered hearts. May Your power be made perfect in our weakness."
